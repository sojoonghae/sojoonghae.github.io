About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the SPADE Lab at UNIST, working at the intersection of chemical engineering and carbon neutrality. My research uses data — not only first-principles engineering — to model, control, and optimize chemical and energy processes, and I am particularly interested in how to bridge data gaps when the measurements needed for modeling or control are scarce.

Looking further ahead, I want to connect these methods through multi-scale modeling, carrying a process from simulation toward scale-up, demonstration, and eventually the commercialization of sustainable technologies.
